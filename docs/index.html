Methodology

This site is a transparent benchmark of a GB battery (BESS) trading strategy. It is not a live trading account: no real money, broker, or exchange is involved, and no orders are ever placed. Every figure is the output of a settlement engine run over published market data, so the rules and assumptions behind each day's PnL are fully stated below.

Reference assets

The benchmark tracks a single 50 MW battery at three storage durations, run side by side:

  • 1-hour — 50 MW / 50 MWh
  • 2-hour — 50 MW / 100 MWh
  • 4-hour — 50 MW / 200 MWh

The power rating is fixed at 50 MW across all three; only the energy capacity (and therefore the discharge duration) changes.

Data sources

  • ENTSO-E — the GB day-ahead auction price used to schedule the day-ahead trades.
  • Elexon — the Market Index Data (MID) price used for intraday re-optimisation, plus generation and demand data for day-type context.

How a day is traded

The day-ahead schedule is optimised against the actual cleared day-ahead price for the delivery day. That price is published the day before, around 11:00, so optimising against it is not lookahead — it is information a real participant would have held before the delivery day began.

During the delivery day, the schedule is re-optimised intraday against the observed MID price, allowing the strategy to adjust to conditions that differ from the day-ahead expectation.

Frictions modelled

The benchmark is not frictionless. The reused settlement engine charges:

  • Slippage on executed trades.
  • Degradation cost reflecting the wear of cycling the battery.

Out of scope

To keep the benchmark honest about what it does and does not measure, the following are explicitly not included:

  • No real execution or order book — trades are settled, not placed.
  • No imbalance settlement.
  • No broker or exchange fees beyond the frictions listed above.

Results are illustrative of strategy performance under the stated assumptions. They are not a guarantee of replicable real-world trading results.
